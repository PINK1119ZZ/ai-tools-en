Why Do People Leave Cloudways?

Before picking an alternative, it's worth being honest about what you're running from. Not every "Cloudways problem" is actually a Cloudways problem:

  • Price: $11/month minimum before you can host anything. For a low-traffic personal site, that's expensive. Competitors like UltaHost start at $3.29/month with similar managed features.
  • No email hosting: Cloudways explicitly doesn't include email. You need a separate Rackspace/Mailgun setup, which confuses beginners and adds cost.
  • Acquisition concerns: DigitalMesh acquired Cloudways in 2022. Some users worry the product roadmap will shift or pricing will increase post-acquisition.
  • Overage costs: Bandwidth, storage, and app overages add up. The "$11/month" can balloon to $30+ for active sites.
  • Want raw control: The managed layer limits what you can configure at the server level. Power users hit walls.
  • Support quality: Multiple users report longer wait times and less-experienced agents since 2023.

With that in mind, here's who you're actually choosing between.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest alternative to Cloudways in 2026?
UltaHost is the cheapest managed alternative at $3.29/month — about 70% cheaper than Cloudways' $11/month entry price. For raw VPS (unmanaged), Vultr starts at $2.50/month. DigitalOcean offers the best balance of price and ecosystem starting at $4/month.
Is DigitalOcean a good alternative to Cloudways?
For developers, yes — it's the infrastructure Cloudways actually runs on, at 40–60% lower cost. You lose the managed WordPress layer, but gain full server control. For non-technical WordPress users, UltaHost is a better Cloudways alternative because it's still fully managed.
Why do people leave Cloudways?
The most common reasons: pricing ($11/month minimum is steep for small sites), no email hosting included, overage costs on bandwidth/storage, support quality concerns post-DigitalMesh acquisition, and wanting raw server control without the managed layer restrictions.
Is WP Engine better than Cloudways?
For WordPress-only workloads: WP Engine wins on WordPress-specific features, developer tooling, and local dev environment. Cloudways wins on flexibility (non-WordPress apps), pricing, and multi-cloud choice. WP Engine starts at $20/month vs Cloudways' $11/month.
Can I migrate from Cloudways easily?
Yes. Most alternatives offer free migration services. UltaHost and SiteGround include free migrations for all plans. DigitalOcean has community guides for migrating WordPress from Cloudways. Budget 2–4 hours for migration and 24–48 hours for DNS propagation if changing nameservers.
What is the best Cloudways alternative for WordPress speed?
Kinsta is the fastest for high-traffic WordPress (Google Cloud C2 + full-page cache + CDN). For the best price-to-speed ratio, UltaHost's NVMe + LiteSpeed setup consistently delivers sub-350ms TTFB at a fraction of Kinsta's cost.
