What Are These Platforms?

Both Systeme.io and ClickFunnels are all-in-one marketing platforms designed to help you build sales funnels, run email campaigns, sell online courses, and generate revenue — without needing a web developer or a stack of 10 different tools.

Systeme.io in 60 Seconds

Founded in 2018 by Aurélien Amacker, Systeme.io was built with one mission: give solopreneurs every tool they need at a price that doesn't require VC funding. The platform offers:

  • Sales funnel builder (drag-and-drop)
  • Email marketing with unlimited sends (on paid plans)
  • Online course creation and delivery
  • Membership sites
  • Blog and website builder
  • Affiliate program management
  • Automation workflows
  • A/B testing
  • A completely free plan — forever

ClickFunnels in 60 Seconds

ClickFunnels was launched in 2014 by Russell Brunson and was a genuine pioneer of the funnel-builder category. For years, it was the de-facto choice for anyone serious about building digital sales infrastructure. Its strengths are its brand recognition, a massive community (the "Two Comma Club" is a clever cult of sorts), and deep integrations with Stripe, PayPal, and major email tools. The weaknesses? The price tag has ballooned while competitors have caught up on features.

⚠️ ClickFunnels 2.0 Warning ClickFunnels released version 2.0 with major changes to the platform. Many existing users experienced bugs, pricing confusion, and forced migrations. This comparison covers CF 2.0 pricing and features as of April 2026.

📧 Email Marketing: Systeme.io Has a Structural Advantage

This is one of the most underappreciated differences between the two platforms. Systeme.io includes a full email marketing suite — sequences, broadcasts, tagging, segmentation, automation rules — built into every plan, with unlimited email sends on all paid tiers.

ClickFunnels historically required a separate email tool (ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, Drip) to handle serious email sequences. Their "Follow-Up Funnels" (formerly Actionetics) is now included in CF 2.0, but it's notably more limited compared to dedicated email platforms, and meaningful automation still requires higher-tier plans.

Practical Impact

Let's say you have a 5,000-person email list and you want to run a 7-day nurture sequence, tag buyers automatically, and launch a broadcast campaign to non-openers. On Systeme.io's $27/month Starter plan, that's all included. On ClickFunnels at $97/month, you'd likely need to also pay for an email platform — adding another $30–$79/month.

🔑 Email is your most valuable marketing asset Don't pay separately for what Systeme.io bundles in. Every dollar you save on SaaS is a dollar you can put into your list.

🔄 Funnel Builder & Templates

This is where ClickFunnels still has a legitimate edge — but it's narrowing. ClickFunnels' library of proven funnel templates is larger, more battle-tested, and built around Russell Brunson's methodology (Value Ladder, Dotcom Secrets framework). If you're following that playbook, those templates can feel like plug-and-play conversion machines.

Systeme.io's template library is growing fast and covers all the essentials: lead capture funnels, tripwire offers, webinar registration, product launch sequences, membership upsells. For most businesses, the templates are more than sufficient.

Builder Experience

Both use drag-and-drop editors. ClickFunnels has historically had a snappier editor with more granular design control. Systeme.io's editor is slightly simpler — which is actually a feature for users who don't want to spend 4 hours on pixel-perfect layouts. You're here to sell, not design.

The reality: 80% of conversion lift comes from your offer and copy, not the pixel-level design of your funnel. Both tools handle the core mechanics well enough that your funnel builder choice shouldn't be the deciding factor.

🎓 Online Courses & Membership Sites

Both platforms now offer fully functional course builders with video hosting, drip content, progress tracking, and membership tiers. This space used to be dominated by Teachable and Thinkific, but all-in-one platforms have made serious inroads.

Systeme.io's course feature is straightforward: upload videos, organize into modules and lessons, drip content on a schedule, sell via your funnel. For most course creators, it's all you need. ClickFunnels 2.0 has a similar offering, though some creators report it's still a bit rough around the edges post-migration.

💡 The Killer Combo: Systeme.io + DataCamp If you're building a course around data skills, AI, or tech — consider pairing Systeme.io (your sales and delivery infrastructure) with DataCamp's curriculum as your content backbone. Use DataCamp to master the subject; use Systeme.io to sell what you know.

🔌 Integrations & Ecosystem

ClickFunnels has a decade of integrations built up. Zapier, Make, major CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), payment processors, webinar tools — the ecosystem is mature. If you're running a larger operation with existing tech stack, ClickFunnels likely has a native integration or documented Zapier flow for it.

Systeme.io integrates with Stripe, PayPal, Razorpay, major SMTP providers, Zapier, and Pabbly Connect. It's leaner, but for most solopreneurs, that's perfectly sufficient. The things that require 15 integrations in other setups often work natively in Systeme.io because everything's already in one place.

Hosting Your Infrastructure

Both platforms are SaaS — you don't host them. But if you're also running side projects (landing pages for clients, dev environments, APIs), consider pairing either platform with cloud hosting. DigitalOcean starts at $4/month for a reliable Droplet, and Cloudways offers managed cloud hosting with better performance than most shared hosting at competitive prices.
