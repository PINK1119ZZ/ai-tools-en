7 Best Platforms to Sell Digital Products in 2026 (Ranked for Creators)

Selling digital products has never been more accessible — or more confusing. In 2026 there are dozens of platforms that let you sell ebooks, templates, courses, memberships, and software. The differences that matter — transaction fees, checkout UX, email tools, affiliate management — are buried in pricing pages designed to obscure the real cost.

The 7 Best Platforms, Ranked

#1 BEST FOR BEGINNERS Gumroad Zero friction, instant start 8.8 / 10

Gumroad is still the default starting point for creators in 2026, and for good reason: you can go from zero to a live product page in under 10 minutes, with no monthly fee. Upload a file, set a price, share the link. That's genuinely it.

The platform handles EU VAT, payment processing, file delivery, and basic affiliate management automatically. In 2026, Gumroad added AI-generated product descriptions and a redesigned storefront — the creator page now looks polished enough that you don't need a separate website to feel professional.

The fee math: Gumroad charges a flat 10% on every sale. On a $20 product, that's $2 per sale. On a $100 product, $10. For a creator doing $500/month in sales, that's $50 in fees — totally reasonable. At $5,000/month, you're paying $500/month to Gumroad, which is when switching starts making sense.

Pros

  • Zero monthly cost — pay only when you sell
  • Live in minutes, no tech required
  • Handles VAT, tax compliance automatically
  • Built-in affiliate program
  • Supports: ebooks, courses, memberships, software, physical goods
  • Clean, trustworthy checkout experience

Cons

  • 10% fee gets expensive at scale
  • Limited email marketing (basic newsletters only)
  • No full funnel or automation builder
  • Course hosting is functional but not polished
  • No upsells or order bumps on free plan

#2 BEST ALL-IN-ONE Systeme.io Full business stack, free plan included 9.1 / 10

Systeme.io is the most underrated platform on this list. For the price of free, you get sales funnels, email automations, course hosting, membership sites, affiliate management, and a website builder — all integrated in one dashboard. This is the tool stack that would cost $300–$500/month if you assembled it from separate services (ClickFunnels + Mailchimp + Teachable + ThriveCart).

The free plan supports up to 2,000 contacts, 3 sales funnels, 1 course, and unlimited emails. For most creators starting out, that's everything they need for the first 12 months of business. There are zero transaction fees on all plans.

Where it shines: Email automation sequences are genuinely powerful. You can set up abandoned cart emails, post-purchase upsells, drip courses, and evergreen webinar funnels without touching any third-party tool. For coaches and course creators, this matters a lot — email is still how digital product businesses make real money.

Pros

  • 0% transaction fees on all plans
  • Free plan is genuinely useful (not crippled)
  • Full email automation built in
  • Course + membership + funnel in one tool
  • Affiliate program with automatic payouts
  • A/B testing on funnels

Cons

  • UI feels dated compared to Kajabi or Podia
  • Course player UX is functional, not beautiful
  • Blog/SEO tools are basic
  • Advanced automations require Webinar plan ($47/mo)
  • Less name recognition for checkout trust

Replace ClickFunnels, Teachable, and Mailchimp — for free.

#3 BEST BUDGET OPTION Payhip Low fees, no-nonsense storefront 8.2 / 10

Payhip sits between Gumroad and Systeme.io: lower transaction fees than Gumroad (5% on the free plan, 2% on Plus, 0% on Pro), with a cleaner storefront than Systeme.io's checkout pages. It supports digital downloads, online courses, memberships, coaching, and physical products from a single dashboard.

The EU VAT handling, coupon codes, affiliate program, and pay-what-you-want pricing are all included on the free plan — features Gumroad gates behind volume or that other platforms charge monthly for. If you're selling digital downloads and don't need a full funnel builder, Payhip's free plan with 5% fees is often the better deal vs Gumroad's 10%.

Pros

  • Lower fees than Gumroad (5% free, 0% on Pro)
  • Clean, embeddable checkout widgets
  • Pay-what-you-want pricing supported
  • EU VAT handled automatically
  • Course and membership features on all plans

Cons

  • Less brand recognition than Gumroad at checkout
  • Email features are very basic
  • No funnel builder or automation
  • Pro plan ($99/mo) needed for 0% fees — expensive for low volume
#4 BEST FOR HIGH VOLUME / SAAS Lemon Squeezy Merchant of record, low flat fees 8.5 / 10

Lemon Squeezy acts as the merchant of record for your sales — meaning they handle all global tax compliance (VAT, GST, sales tax) automatically across every country. For SaaS founders and high-volume digital product sellers, this is the killer feature: you don't need a tax lawyer or manual Stripe Tax configuration.

Pricing starts at $29/month with a 3.5% + 30¢ transaction fee (Starter), dropping to 0.5% + 30¢ on the $79/month Plus plan. If you're doing $5,000+/month in sales, the math on Lemon Squeezy vs Gumroad makes the $79/month fee obvious. Lemon Squeezy also has native license key delivery — great for software and plugin sales.

Pros

  • Merchant of record — zero tax headaches globally
  • License key + software delivery built in
  • Very low fees at higher tiers
  • Strong checkout conversion (fast, modern UI)
  • Subscription billing and SaaS metering

Cons

  • Monthly fee required — not free to start
  • No course hosting or email automation
  • Overkill for simple ebook / template sellers
  • Dashboard is less intuitive for non-technical sellers

#5 BEST COURSE + COMMUNITY Podia Courses, communities, and email in one 8.0 / 10

Podia is the most polished all-in-one course platform for creators who want a beautiful student experience. The course player, community features, and email marketing are all noticeably more refined than Systeme.io — but you pay for it: plans start at $39/month with a 8% transaction fee on the free tier, dropping to 0% on paid plans.

Where Podia stands out is community. Built-in discussion boards, member profiles, and live event hosting make it the best choice for creators building a paid community alongside their courses. Think cohort-based learning, mastermind groups, or membership communities — Podia handles all of it without a separate Circle.so or Discord integration.

Pros

  • Most polished student/member experience
  • Community features built in (no third-party tool)
  • Full email marketing on all paid plans
  • 0% transaction fees on paid plans
  • Great customer support

Cons

  • $39/month minimum for 0% fees
  • No funnel builder or complex automations
  • More expensive than Systeme.io for equivalent features
  • 8% fee on free plan is punishing
#6 BEST FOR VIDEO COURSES Teachable The original course platform 7.8 / 10

Teachable pioneered the online course platform category and still has the most mature video course infrastructure. The lecture builder, drip scheduling, student progress tracking, and certificate generation are all excellent. In 2026, Teachable added AI-assisted course outline generation and automated subtitle generation — genuinely useful for creators who produce a lot of video content.

The free plan has a 10% transaction fee (same as Gumroad) but is limited to one course. Paid plans start at $59/month and remove the fee cap. For creators whose primary product is a structured video course, Teachable's student experience is hard to beat. For everyone else, the price/feature ratio isn't as compelling as Systeme.io or Payhip.

Pros

  • Best-in-class video course player and UX
  • AI course outline and subtitle generation
  • Mature platform with strong brand trust
  • Detailed student analytics
  • Certificate of completion generation

Cons

  • 10% fee on free plan, $59/mo for 0%
  • Email marketing requires third-party integration
  • No funnel builder
  • Weaker for non-course digital products
#7 BEST FOR FULL CONTROL Self-Hosted (WooCommerce + Stripe) Maximum control, lowest long-term fees 7.5 / 10

Running your own store via WooCommerce (or a headless Stripe integration) on a VPS gives you zero platform lock-in, full control over customer data, and the lowest ongoing fees at scale — just Stripe's 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction. There's no platform taking a cut, no plan limits, no risk of a Gumroad-style fee hike.

The trade-off is setup complexity. You need a server, a WordPress install (or custom stack), the WooCommerce plugins for digital delivery and licensing, and someone to maintain it. For developers and high-volume sellers, this is absolutely worth it. For everyone else, the setup overhead isn't justified until you're doing serious volume.

For hosting, DigitalOcean Droplets ($6–12/month) are the cleanest option for a lean WooCommerce setup. If you want managed WordPress without the server admin, Cloudways gives you a fully managed server with automatic backups for ~$14/month — still far cheaper than Teachable or Podia at scale.

Pros

  • Full ownership of customer data and storefront
  • Lowest fees at high volume (2.9% Stripe only)
  • No platform risk (fee hikes, shutdowns)
  • Unlimited products, funnels, customization
  • Full SEO control

Cons

  • Setup requires technical knowledge
  • You handle security, updates, backups
  • Plugin costs add up (WooCommerce extensions)
  • Tax compliance requires Stripe Tax or TaxJar
  • Not a beginner option

Final Verdict: Which Platform Wins in 2026?

There's no single winner — it depends entirely on where you are in your creator journey:

  • Day 1 creator: Gumroad. No risk, no monthly cost, instant launch.
  • Building a course or coaching business: Systeme.io. The free plan is genuinely the best all-in-one deal in the market right now.
  • Selling software or SaaS: Lemon Squeezy. Merchant of record + subscription billing makes it the only sensible choice.
  • Growing fast, want low fees + community: Podia at $39/month gives you 0% fees and a community platform in one.
  • High volume, want full ownership: Self-hosted on DigitalOcean + Stripe pays for itself quickly.
