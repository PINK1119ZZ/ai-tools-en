🔍 What Is NordVPN? (And Why Does Everyone Recommend It?)

NordVPN is a commercial VPN service launched in 2012 by Nord Security, a Panama-based company. It's grown into one of the world's largest VPN providers with over 6,300 servers across 111 countries and more than 14 million users.

The "Nord" brand has since expanded into a whole suite of security products — NordPass (password manager), NordLocker (encrypted cloud storage), and NordLayer (business VPN). But the core VPN product is what made them famous, and it's still their flagship.

Why is it always in every "best VPN" list? Because it consistently delivers on the three things that matter most: speed, security, and reliability. While many VPNs are good at one or two, NordVPN has managed to nail all three without charging a premium for it.

Key fact: NordVPN's servers run on RAM-only infrastructure — no data is ever written to physical hard drives. Even if a server were seized by authorities, there'd be nothing to recover. This isn't marketing fluff — it's been verified by independent auditors.

🔐 Security & Privacy: Does NordVPN Actually Protect You?

This is where NordVPN genuinely earns its reputation. Let's go through the key security claims and what the evidence actually says:

No-Logs Policy — Verified, Not Just Claimed

NordVPN's no-logs policy has been independently audited four times — twice by PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) and twice by Deloitte. These aren't self-reported claims; they're third-party forensic audits that examined their server infrastructure and internal processes.

In 2018, a Finnish server was compromised. The attacker got access to the server — but found nothing useful because no user data was stored. This real-world incident actually proved their no-logs policy works in practice, not just on paper.

Threat Protection Pro

This is a genuinely useful extra. Threat Protection Pro (included in Plus/Ultimate plans) works as a built-in ad blocker, malware scanner, and tracker blocker — and it works even when the VPN isn't connected. It scans files you download before they run, blocks malicious websites at DNS level, and strips tracking parameters from URLs. For a lot of users, this alone replaces separate security software.

Double VPN & Onion Over VPN

For high-sensitivity use cases, NordVPN offers specialized server configurations: Double VPN routes your traffic through two servers in two different countries, and Onion Over VPN routes it through the Tor network. These are significantly slower but essentially untrackable — useful for journalists, activists, or anyone operating under a repressive regime.

🛠️ Key Features Breakdown

6,300+ Servers in 111 Countries

NordVPN's server coverage is among the largest in the industry. More importantly, they've been expanding specialty servers: P2P-optimized servers for torrenting, obfuscated servers for bypassing VPN detection in countries like China or UAE, and dedicated IP servers for users who need a consistent IP address.

Meshnet — The Hidden Gem Feature

Meshnet lets you create a private network between your own devices or with trusted contacts — essentially a personal VPN. It's free even on the Basic plan. Useful for accessing your home network remotely, gaming with friends on a LAN-style connection, or securely sharing files. Most users don't know this exists, but it's surprisingly powerful.

Split Tunneling

Available on Windows and Android, split tunneling lets you route specific apps through the VPN while others use your regular connection. Practical example: stream Netflix (which sometimes blocks VPNs) on your direct connection while keeping your browser traffic routed through NordVPN.

Obfuscated Servers for Censored Regions

If you're in China, Russia, UAE, or any country that blocks VPNs, NordVPN's obfuscated servers disguise VPN traffic to look like regular HTTPS traffic. This is a niche feature but a life-saver when you need it — and NordVPN's implementation is one of the more reliable ones we've tested.

📱 Apps & User Experience: What's It Actually Like to Use?

NordVPN has polished apps across all major platforms — Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, Linux, and browser extensions for Chrome and Firefox. There's also a smart TV app and Fire TV stick support.

Mobile Apps (iOS & Android)

The mobile apps are clean and fast. The Quick Connect button does what it says — one tap, fastest available server. The map interface looks great but isn't super practical for finding specific servers (use the search bar instead). Battery impact is minimal compared to older VPN protocols.

Desktop Apps (Windows & macOS)

Windows has the most features (split tunneling, full Threat Protection, all specialty servers). macOS is good but lacks split tunneling — this is a meaningful gap for power users. Both apps are stable and rarely crash.

Linux

A proper GUI app launched for Linux in 2023, replacing the old command-line-only experience. Still behind Windows in features but much more accessible than it used to be. NordLynx is available on Linux since kernel 5.6+.

Router Support

NordVPN supports DD-WRT, Tomato, OpenWRT, and ASUS routers. This lets you protect every device on your network (including smart TVs and gaming consoles that don't support VPN apps natively). The setup is more complex than ExpressVPN's router app, but doable with their guides.
